Domino’s Pizza, Pilawoos Cheese Kottu, and now hot dogs from the ‘Dawg House’, this has not been a week of healthful and high-brow culinary exertion. Still it all needs reviewing and the basic Elephant House hot dog is an enduringly popular feature of Colombo’s fast-food landscape. Though I’ve never really been able to work out why.

Half-processed pig off cuts and various E-numbers rolled into a somewhat phallic shape, stuffed into one of those cheap over-sweet buns and covered in that famous but troublingly coloured Elephant House sauce. I’m not a big fan. Especially that sauce, people really love the stuff (I think it’s a nostalgia thing) but honestly what is it? It’s not chilli sauce, not mustard, not mayonnaise, ketchup or any discernible kind of chutney, and why does it so closely resemble a certain troubling and unwelcome bodily fluid ? I suppose there are things man wasn’t meant to know.

What I do know is that for all their culinary deficiencies E-House hotdogs are inexpensive and fast. A basic ‘dawg’ - one sausage in a bun covered in that gloop is Rs 100, and will be plonked in front of you in about 10 seconds. So for immediate hunger cessation not a bad option though the runny, yellow sauce means New York style eating as you go isn’t a good idea. Better to sit yourself down and get the business over with. I dropped into the Odel food-court outlet and ordered a jumbo dawg (it was breakfast and lunch in one) Rs 145.

It’s a sausage in a bun so a detailed description of the subtle affects its few flavors had on my taste buds isn’t really possible. Still Elephant House sausages aren’t that bad, you can imagine that pork makes up a good percentage of what you’re eating, don’t think about what constitutes the other percentage, and the indefinable, sweet, watery gloop, for all its aesthetic deficiencies does add something, though are those bit of dhal in it, if so why? The one real issue is that the sausage to meat ratio seems to deteriorate down the lengh of the bun and by the end youre pretty much chomping on pure, dry cotton-wool type bread, mmm… tasteless.

This really isn’t food for me – but I’ll concede it goes down pretty easily and its fast and cheap. They have some other choices besides dawgs- a sort of KFC style chicken burger Rs 200 and a more advanced looking chilli cheese dog. Im not really sure Elephant House dawgs should be getting that ambitious- the plane dawg though is fine and washed down with some old school Elephant House soft drink (go for Orange Crush) it’s an insta-trip down memory lane.

TIP

Must be eaten with an Elephant House soft drink.
