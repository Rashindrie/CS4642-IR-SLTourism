Harpo's Pizza generally serves up some of the best pies in the city, with fresh toppings and a wide range.
They've recently launched their Online Delivery site, so we thought we'd check that out during a special lunch sesh with the editorial team, but our experience wasn't as breezy as we thought it'd be.


The Site

The fancy new website portal is www.harpospizza.com. You're required to sign in/sign up before you can place your order, and you'll get a confirmation number to your mobile phone. 


The interface is quite alright, with rather bright colours and a categorised menu for easy access. It's easy , all you do is select your pizza, the size, and then it'll be added to your cart. Same goes for any other item you'd like to order (pizzas, sides, desserts etc).

Once you're done ordering, you go to your cart and then enter in your details, where you'd like to get it delivered etc.

Once you've made your decision, you confirm your order and then you're directed to the payment options and choose between card and cash.

Note: They have some excellent deals on their websites, so make sure you check them out. 

The Pizzas

We took the Lunch Deal they had on offer, which means you order a 12" pizza of your choice and get a regular 9" pizza (there are only a couple of choices, of course) for free. 



The two pizzas you can choose as your free pizza, are the Margherita and a Pizza Pollo.
For our main, we got the Pizza Pancetta and Parmigiana (Pork) which comes with crispy bacon mozarella and parmesan on a tomato base for Rs. 1700. The pizza was delicious with the mozarella and parmesan coming through perfectly. None of the ingredients overpowered each other and the tomato base was light and tangy.

The Pizza Pollo (Chicken) was so good I would've paid more just to have a 12" to myself. It comes with bits of spicy chicken, roast capsicum, sauteed onions along with mozarella cheese on a tomato base. The chicken wasn't that spicy but I didn't mind. The onions were nice and crunchy and surprisingly enough didn't overthrow the pizza. 

For dessert, we got the Apple Crumble Pizza (Rs. 600). Normally the idea of a dessert pizza sounds off to me, but I didn't dislike this. The apple was nice and sweet and the whole pizza had a slight cinnamon tinge to it. It also came with a light spread of nutella at the base, which we loved. Texturally this is a treat, with squashy nutella, crispy crumble, and lightly toasted pizza base.

Service

We were seriously craving good pizza and drink on a gloomy afternoon, but of course life is never as simple as that.
Once we ordered, we were sent a confirmation message through the site telling us that our pizzas would be delivered shortly.
About an hour later with no sign of the pizzas, we called them up and after a lengthy conversation they tell us that the orders haven't gone through due to an error in their internet connection. They told us that it'd take another 45 minutes for them to deliver and we were having second thoughts about whether it was worth waiting 2+ hours for a pizza. We caved since we were so hungry and it was 3 PM at this point and most lunch spots were closed, plus they told us the pizzas were on the house due to the inconvenience. 
We appreciated the apologetic service, but still, nobody needs to wait 2+ hours for a pizza so that's a minus point from me.

Conclusion

The pizzas (when they finally arrived) were hot, and delicious. We were told the site glitch wouldn't happen again, but if you want to make sure your slice is on the way, maybe stick to the classic telephone order instead.


TIP

Try the Apple Crumble dessert pizza.

    Review From November 2014

    Reviewed by Bhagya
    3 years ago
    Harpo's Pizza does some pretty great pizzas using high quality ingredients. They're not cheap, but the pizzas are authentic and  you get more bang for your buck than most other places.

    The Pizzas

    When it comes to the menu, Harpo's isn't the most extensive but they've still got around 25 pizzas to choose from, which is not bad at all. While they may not have the largest selection, they've definitely got some of the most interesting combinations. They've got a great selection of meats like mutton, lamb and yes, they do serve pork based products, so everything from bacon, to salami, to parma ham is available. Many pizzerias nowadays don't serve pork, in order to to maintain Halal standards, but you can't really be authentic unless you do. When compared to others, Harpo's is definitely more expensive but you could probably attribute that to the quality of ingredients. The 9' pizzas range from Rs. 960 - Rs. 1460, with the 12' pizzas costing roughly Rs. 500 more. They've also got a massive 19' pizza option as well at Rs. 3250 upwards, which comes with 16 slices.

    We got the pizza grecia (Rs. 1060), since we wanted to see how their lamb pizzas fare. It comes with rosemary coated lamb, black olives, mozzarella and feta cheese. This is the first lamb pizza we've had from any local pizzeria and it was delicious. The lamb was was well seasoned and soft, complemented by the subtle aroma and flavour of the rosemary. I'm not a fan of olives, but coupled with the feta cheese it adds a nice balance of saltiness to the pizza.

    The pizza marinara (Rs. 1110) is their main seafood offering which comes with both prawns and calamari. This one was quite nice, if a bit mild when it comes to spice. This one is basically a simple seafood pizza with mozzarella, garlic and tomatoes, but the quality of the fresh seafood is what really shines through. Only thing I would personally have liked was a bit more spice, but that's a personal preference, and they do send sachets of chilli flakes which you can add.

    We also tried out the pizza romagna (Rs. 1060) which comes with bacon and smoked chicken. I've said it before and I'll say it again, pizzas and bacon just work, add in smoked chicken and mushrooms to the mix and it becomes that much better. This is kinda' unrelated, but the night we got these delivered we were making waffles, so we decided to wrap a pizza slice in a waffle, and it worked brilliantly. So yeah, fluffy waffle + thin crust pizza = gold.

    Delivery & Service

    The delivery, in our case was prompt and came right on time. We had it delivered to Mt.Lavinia which is pretty far from Harpo's, on a rainy day, so we're impressed. Delivery is free within Colombo and Rs. 150 to suburbs, which is pretty reasonable. The gentleman who took our order was very polite and explained the different pizzas when we inquired. So all in all we were very satisfied with the service.

    Conclusion

    Harpo's may be pricier than other pizzerias, but they justify that by delivering some great pizzas with high quality ingredients. For those of you looking for authentic pizzas with pork based products, Harpo's is a very good bet.

    Review From September 2012

    Reviewed by indi

    This review has been long delayed, perhaps because it's so close to home. I am such a frequent customer of Harpo's Pizza that they, thoughtfully, have called to thank me and invite me for stuff. So it's pretty good. We're including this as a service, but it's really delivery (4869000), free in Colombo 1-15, Rs. 100 to the suburbs.

    Harpo's Pizza is a spin-off from Bayleaf Restaurant, though they also have an outlet at ODEL and pizza available at the Colombo Fort Cafe. Harpo's makes pizza which is cheaper or not much more than commercial outlets like Pizza Hut and Domino's while being a whole lot better. That said, the pizza used to be great and now consistency varies. When you're hungry and the kitchen is terrifying, however, Harpo's pizza delivery can't be beat.

    Some recommendations. We've given prices for the 12 inch size which serves two but which, honestly, I can eat by myself.
    • Pizza Grecia (Rs. 1300): their lamb pizza is innovative and, if you like lamb, good. Perhaps that's redundant. It's one of my favorites.
    • Pizza Tikka (Rs. 1300): Another innovation, this takes Indian style tikka chicken and puts it on a pizza. Used to be amazing, still pretty good.
    • Pizza Marinara (Rs. 1480) or Utopia: if you like seafood, these are some good seafood pizza options.
    All in all, Harpo's Pizza is still a classic (though only five years old). They combine strange and interesting ingredients with a highly professional kitchen and delivery outfit. If you're having a party or working late or just don't want to cook, Harpo's Pizza still can't be beat.

    ஹார்போஸ் பிட்ஸா; பேலீப் உணவகத்தின் சகோதர உணவகமாகும். இங்கு மலிவான நல்ல பிட்ஸாக்களை சுவைக்கமுடியும். ஆனாலும் தரம் அடிக்கடி மாறுவதையும் சொல்லித்தான் ஆகவேண்டும். இவர்களுக்கு "ஒடேல்" இல் ஒரு கிளை இருக்கும் அதேவேளை கொழும்பு போர்ட் கபேயிலும் இவர்களின் பிட்சா கிடைக்கிறது. இந்த உணவகம் பற்றிய விமர்சனம் இவ்வளவு நாள் பிந்தியதற்கு காரணம் இது எனது வீட்டிற்கு மிக அருகில் இருப்பதாகும்.

    (நாம் சொல்லும் விலைகள் எல்லாம் 12 இன்ச் அளவு பிட்சாக்களினது என்பதை கவனத்தில் கொள்ளவும். அது இரண்டுபேர் சாப்பிடுவதற்கான அளவு என்றாலும் ஒருவரால் தனியாக சாப்பிடவும் முடியும்.)
    • பிட்ஸா கிரேஸியா (ரூபா 1,300): நீங்கள் ஆட்டிறைச்சியை (லேம்ப்) விரும்புபவராயின் இந்த புதுமையான பிட்ஸா உங்களுக்கு பிடிக்கும். எனக்கு விருப்பமானதொன்று.
    • பிட்சா டிக்கா (ரூபா 1,300): இதுவும் இவர்களின் புதுமையான படைப்பு. இந்திய டிக்கா சமையல் முறையில் சமைக்கப்பட்ட கோழி இறைச்சி போடப்பட்டது. அருமையானது.
    • பிட்ஸா மரினாரா / உதோபியா (ருபா 1,480): நீங்கள் கடல் உணவுகளை விரும்புபவர்களாயின் உங்களுக்கான நல்ல தெரிவு இது.
    ஒட்டுமொத்தமாக பார்க்கையில்; ஐந்து ஆண்டுகளே வயதான ஹார்போஸ் பிட்ஸா தரமான பிட்ஸாக்களைத்தருகின்றது. அதுமட்டுமல்லாது டெலிவரி கூட நல்லதொரு தரத்தில் இருக்கின்றது. கொழும்பு 1 முதல் 15 வரையான இடங்களுக்கு இலவசமாகவும் அயற்பிரதேசங்களுக்கு ரூபா 100 கட்டணத்திலும் ஹோம் டெலிவரி சேவை கிடைக்கிறது (அழைக்க - 4869000). உங்கள் வீட்டில் ஏதேனும் கொண்டாட்டமா, நேரஞ்சென்று வீட்டிற்கு வருகிறீர்களா அல்லது சமைக்க சோம்பலாக இருக்கின்றதா? ஹார்போஸ் பீட்ஸாவை முயற்சித்து பாருங்கள்.
