Golden Mile has closed and been replaced by Shore By O!

There are a slew of restaurants on Mt Lavinia Beach, and the Golden Mile, in my mind offers a sense of sea-faring adventure that you won’t find anywhere else on the strip. The whole place is all rustic and salty appearing as if an ancient ship had run aground. You make your way up to the top dining area and seat yourself on the “first deck” where you can relax and take it all in while sipping a Lion Lager.

The venue manager personally waited on us during a very quiet night and his attention to detail and management of his staff made for a very enjoyable evening. The whole staff stayed behind well after closing time for our benefit while we ordered several more beers.

The menu had a great variety for all palettes and had a heroic seafood section that had us salivating at its promising bounty. We ordered the chilli crab dish and a spicy prawn dish as main and the Hot Butter Cuttlefish for starters. As we waited for the food, rain began sprinkling gently over the beach.

Now, I can’t stress this enough. Order the Hot Butter Cuttlefish. It will become an addiction and like me you will also search desperately for it on every menu you ever encounter. But that stuff is amazing. I guess, as a dish, its kind difficult to mess up unless you over cook the cuttlefish or over batter it. This is not the case at the Golden Mile and what arrived was a beautiful golden mound of lightly battered well-cooked cuttlefish. Onions, chillies, garlic, and greens contrasted the gold fried cephalopod and flavoured it perfectly as it pleasantly fired up my taste buds and sizzled down my throat.

A few beers later, along comes the mains. Now maybe it’s the expectations set by an Australian upbringing and too many episodes of Masterchef that I would never admit to, but generally when I order seafood, I’m always resoundingly disappointed by the size of the portions. Imagine my sheer state of joy when a mountain of prawns appeared in front of me, accompanied by a nice little mound of rice and a copse of salad. The crabs that were also ordered stood over my plate like Jupiter over Earth.

Unfortunately I have to admit a collective defeat. I demolished my delicious prawns and attempted to help out with the crabs, but despite my taste buds collectively reeling from the goodness, we had to leave some of the crab behind. After dinner we settled in for a few more lagers and chatted away till well past midnight. I feel this is important to mention for the generous hospitality we received. The manager kept serving us and took the time to have a chat about his broken wrist, which I hope is healed by now. When it started pouring down outside, we were ushered inside and ashtrays were brought to us so that we could continue our conversation and finish our drinks.

There was a moment when out of the blue, a clatter came from the kitchen followed closely by a junior chef and bus boy who wrestled each other in the opposing dining room. All credit to the manager for dealing with this staff dispute quickly and effectively and completely playing down the incident for our benefit, resuming quickly to normal service and professional demeanour.

I’d highly recommend The Golden Mile and I myself have returned there since my first experience at this venue.

Review by Seshanka Samarajiwa
