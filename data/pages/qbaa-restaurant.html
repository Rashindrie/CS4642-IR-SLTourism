Qbaa is one of the more mature bars in Colombo, and they also house a fully fledged restaurant which is actually pretty great.

The Bar & Drinks

While it does have a separate restaurant Qbaa is still a bar at its core. A couple of years ago it used to be one of the most popular spots, especially if you were a fan of live music. Nowadays the crowds are a lot less but the music, on the day we went, was still pretty darn good.

The bar itself is spacious, with barstools, tables and couches for seating. It’s also good if you’re not a fan of people constantly taking pictures of themselves since the place is very dimly lit.

The bahama mama (Rs. 780) is a mix of white rum, red rum and coffee liqueur, which makes for a hell of a cocktail. This was easily one of the most alcoholic cocktails we’ve had at a local bar. It also tastes quite nice with the addition of a bit of pineapple. A couple of these and you’ll be feeling it.

For something a little low key we tried out the old fashioned (Rs. 700) which is a bourbon based, somewhat like a bourbon sour. This one wasn’t as strong as the bahama mama but if you’re a fan of whiskey you’ll probably like this one.

The Restaurant & Food

For a time we thought that Qbaa’s restaurant was closed but it's still open, but only for dinner. The menu isn’t very extensive but they do offer a good range of meats and seafood, with prices ranging from Rs. 800 - Rs. 2000, which isn’t too bad since the portions were actually quite large.

I find that a good bread basket can really set the mood for the rest of meal and the one we got at Qbaa was excellent. It wasn’t too fancy but all of it was warm and seemed freshly baked.

The broiled teriyaki lamb chops (Rs. 1400) came with a side of veges and saffron rice which makes it more than filling. The lamb was cooked somewhere between medium and well done with a good balance of seasoning. We would have preferred the teriyaki sauce to have been a bit thicker but it was fine in terms of flavour.

The mustard pork chops (Rs. 1200) were also very good. This one comes with four sizeable pork chops, slathered in a thick honey mustard sauce served with a smooth mash, boiled veges and a salsa. While the mash and the salsa were good complements to the dish, the real hero was the pork, which was tender and flavoursome with the honey mustard sauce adding a bit of sweetness.

We were pretty full by this point but we thought we’d give their blueberry cheesecake (Rs. 500) a shot, and we’re really glad we did ‘cause it was so darn good. The cheesecake itself was super smooth and creamy, but not too sweet. The blueberry topping was also legit. As in without a doubt one of the best we’ve had with a perfect sweet and sour balance.

Conclusion

Whether it be for a night out with some friends or a three course dinner, Qbaa will most likely leave you pleasantly surprised with interesting drinks, tasty food and pleasant ambience.


TIP

Try that cheesecake, you won't regret it.

RukshanFernando

So you’ve developed a taste for good wines and know your jazz artiste by just the sound of the saxophone. And you appreciate fine food and enjoy a drink on the terrace with your friends who’ve been with you through thick and thin. But still, you have been on the lookout for that one place that offers all this, with a relaxed atmosphere; away from the buzz of a five star hotel and the thumping vibes of heavy metal music. Look no further as the latest restaurant in Colombo, Qbaa, caters to all these requirements through genuine service.

It is the music based theme that sets the mood as you enter the fine dining area of Qbaa. The cozy and chic furniture, the comfy layout and the feeling of airiness exemplifies its interior design by celebrated architect, Murad Ismail. Served by a team of a well trained gracious staff, guests are offered a range of foods from its continental and fusion menus (including Thai, Italian and Greek recipes) using fine meats from Australia and fresh sea-food from Sri Lanka.

Interested in uncorking and unwinding for the evening? Enter Qbaa’s Wine Cellar where you will be greeted by Qbaa’s own sommelier and be introduced to a range of wines from around the world: Bordeaux to Barossa Valley to Stellenbosch and Napa Valley. The snug dim lit room with its wooden racks, filled with affordable to exclusive world renowned Cabernet Sauvignons, Rieslings, Chardonnays, Semillons and more, cater to the varied tastes of wine enthusiasts. So step in and enjoy a casual tasting session or choose your own wine for your fine dining experience. And if you are that keen on wine, keep your eyes peeled for Qbaa’s monthly wine tasting sessions by recognized global brands!

Those preferring quality entertainment should go by its cozy Music Lounge, designed to international standards. Created to meet the dearth of entertainment suiting the palette of those aged 35 and above, it is where you can sit on a couch, sip your choice from the well stacked bar and watch the band play (Tuesday to Sunday). Singer and lyricist Keithan Carter, famed in the Chicago R&B scene, performs his magic as a regular favourite at Qbaa’s Music Lounge, while another regular is one of Sri Lanka’s finest Jazz musicians, Dulip Gnanakan. Other artistes that entertain customers include ‘Glory’ and ‘A Team’ with drum legend, Aruna Siriwardena.

Meanwhile the bar by the Music Lounge serves delightful sandwiches and other snacks including bacon wraps, spare ribs, stuffed crab claws, Chili concarne made using New Zealand Cheddar and Qbaa’s own seafood paella!

Looking for a bit of romance? Sit under a canopy at the Terrace up on the rooftop and enjoy a quiet meal under the starlit sky. It is here where you can also enjoy coffee with friends in the eve, or relax with a book tanning yourself on a sunny afternoon. Or it’s party time at the Terrace above Qbaa, if you wish. You are invited to book early and hold pleasant gatherings, making it an event to remember.

The welcoming smiles and the laid back setting of Qbaa soothes the mind, whether you drop by for lunch or dinner, or just any time of day. A place to relax or treat guests, a place to feel pampered or forget the cares of the world.

SlightlyOffended

Went to Qbaa for the first time a few nights ago with a big group. Foreigners and couple of Sri Lankans. Magically only the 2 Sri Lankans (who grew up overseas and only just moved back to LK last year and can't even speak the local language) had to pay for a LKR1000 'voucher' at the bar, as if insinuating we wouldn't spend that much anyway. Personally I spent about LKR4k on drinks+dinner and would have spent more! Quite disrespectful and a couple in our group were quite offended including myself. We were definitely the only ones made to do that from what I observed through the night.

Apart from that, and a somewhat lack of friendly service from one of the bar staff, its a great place for a hangout. Great food, drinks prices are reasonable given the decor, location and feel. Staff need to be more respectful, friendly, and that voucher thing will lose Qbaa customers for sure due to the attitude it portrays (insults customers by obligating them to spend a minimum…which is approx. SGD10 only lol). Stop that now! The crowd dynamic will handle itself, you don't want to alienate any customers who CAN spend more, based on their appearances! Bad idea, bad policy.

Crowd was mostly over 35, music was great (Thursday night) although we were a bit close to the band so was quite loud (sit further back if you're looking for a chilled conversation or try upstairs on the roof). The female singer was amazing.

Definitely going back to try the dinner menu in the restaurant and have some of my favorite wine from the wine menu (haven't found it anywhere else in SL!). Qbaa is definitely upmarket, but in this day and age the 'snooty' service paradigm is dead. There should be only one customer service standard for ALL customers. Its just nicer doing business that way!

Happy eating!
