Kiito is a bespoke tailor on Rosmead Place. They do excellent bespoke work ranging from suits (Rs. 16,000 upwards) to shirts and trousers (Rs. 3,000 upwards). They have material or you can bring your own. The main thing that makes Kiito our recommendation is the service provided by Damith Cooray and his wife.

Background



Colombo has a lot of affordable tailories (is that the word?) that can turn out pretty good suits and stuff, with direction. But that's the rub. With bespoke tailoring, communication between the client and tailor is key. The awesomely named Gaylord (Galle Road) and Hercules (Havelock Road) can do suits and things, but it's a bit of a chop shop in there, a lot of people coming and going, and to get them to do something to modern tastes takes a lot of effort. Raheem's on Issipathana (off Havelock Road) has a young and voluble manager who you can talk to, but he seems to spend all of his attention talking to the many women that drop in.

Kiito is different cause it's not a bustling chop-shop and Damith is a highly intelligent guy to work with. Most guys actually don't know what they want and he's a more helpful guide than the disinterested guys at, say, Hercules.

The Service



What I've ordered from Damith's is my wedding suit, like four groomsmen's suits, a shirt, and now some non-hobo work clothes that have yet to arrive. I've been quite happy with the results, but moreso with the process, which is kinda what bespoke tailoring is. Damith is basically always available and infinitely amenable to the random requests a wedding generates. For example, he was able to cut up a tie to make into a bow-tie for the little page boy. He was able to adjust my pants to my wife's exacting satisfaction. Et cetera.

Weddings are basically the stress test of a tailor and Kiito's passes. For weddings what you're looking for is someone you can give a job to and then not worry about it (A) and someone who can deal with multiple changes from lots of people (B). Oh, and they also have to deal with the inherent contradiction between A and B. Almost incidental is that the suits need to look good and be of high quality, which they are, but Kiito's goes the extra essential step of making the process as painless as possible.

Basically, he answers the phone, sends SMS if he can't, gives good advice, delivers on time and produces quality stuff. His wife and the staff in general are also lovely to work with.

The Stuff



Onto the suits. I got a linen suit for my wedding, plus four suits for the groomsmen. If you want fabric they have a good selection there, the only thing that's really lacking is linen. I got that from outside. They have a lot of wool, cottons, and even handloom fabric (in swatches). For shirts they have two types of white at any given time and a variety of colored.

The process is roughly that Damith will measure you and then make a rough mockup of the suit, sometimes by the next day. Then you come in, try it on, make any adjustments (I recommend bringing your wife or whatever, to spare coming back later) and then they deliver. They can do orders in 24 hours, but if it's not urgent it takes about a week.

It's modern, generally well stitched and competitive price wise with the far less pleasant main street experiences.

Location



Kiito's is on Rosmead Place. It's a simple location, fabrics, a couch, wedding magazines, and a table where Damith works. There's a full length mirror and you can try stuff on in a back room. It's not super posh, but it's nice.

Conclusion



If you're getting a suit or even shirt/trousers, we recommend Kiito. The prices are comparable to main street tailors and off-the shelf stuff, and the quality and service is much better.

TIP

Check out the handloom jackets. Pretty cool.
