NicNac is a brand new delivery service that's got it's own free app (iOS only, currently). They've got just about 8 drivers for the time being, but they've got a call out for drivers, so it's likely to pick up soon. We placed an order for a bunch of random items and while they took a while to get to us, they did manage to find everything we asked for. 

Ordering

The app's interface is pretty simple, which we appreciate. You have the option of ordering groceries, electronics, laundry, fast food, medicine and even license recovery. None of us have had our licenses confiscated, so we didn't have the need for that. We ordered a few things from each category, but we placed the order by typing it into the groceries option. Our order included an extension cord with a square plug, a box of tissues, a card of Panadol, a passion fruit frappe from Commons and a thambili. You can set the exact time at which you'd like the delivery to be made.

Delivery & Rates

So, they charge Rs 100 for delivery and since we ordered at 1PM, which is rush hour, they charge double. We got a call from the service telling us their drivers are busy and it would take one and a half hours for the delivery. The driver called to confirm what type of extension cord we wanted and how many ports we needed. Then, there was another call when the driver was close by and wanted to confirm the location. Then four guys walked up to our office and delivered the goods, but we reckon it's usually just one dude.

Sadly, the extension cord wasn't a square plug type, but everything else was in order. And considering how odd our order was, we'd say that it's off to a decent start but their delivery time has to be faster.

Conclusion

We think NicNac is a good idea, though Speedee and Quickee will do the same, and the app is functional. They're not very fast, which is the main drawback right now. Having an app to get your laundry done is pretty neat, though.
