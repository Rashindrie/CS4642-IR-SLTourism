Blackbird Brekkie and Brunch

1st Floor, 282 Liberty Arcade, Colpetty.

A plethora of breakfast and brunch options ranging from Sri Lankan, Arab, and British to Italian dishes.

We usually groan when we hear of a place that serves up a bunch of cuisines instead of specialising in one type of fare, but Blackbird put our tentative fears about badly-executed food to rest with what we got. Offering a spread ranging from Sri Lankan to Arab and British breakfasts and lunches, the dishes are wonderfully delivered and a treat to the palate.

Ambience and Service

Following a muted grey and yellow colour scheme, Blackbird is clean and minimal. It's got a hip little counter area of matte wood, and there's a nice balance of old-school warmth and modernity. The space looks misleadingly larger than it is because of a glass wall facing Duplication Road.

Service-wise, the staff are nippy on their feet, friendly, and quick to help with the menu and make recommendations. 

The Experience

Deciding is a bit of a pain, because of the wide variety of options presented helter-skelter on the menu card. Like, do you go for burgers or hummus? Or rice and curry? Why is there carbonara, but then again, why not? Having a multitude of options is usually a good thing except when you need to review: which cuisine do you stick by?

Given that Blackbird seems to be run by a British lady and that the concept of brunching itself is well, British, we settled for Fish and Chips. And a Carbonara just to try out some of the pasta options.

At Rs. 890, our carbonara was a large and filling portion, topped with bits of bacon. I found the pasta itself quite creamy, flavoursome and soft, but my friend tells me that the bacon was barely noticeable because the other flavours overpowered it. It was accompanied by a chunk of toasted and crunchy roast-paan as well.

Priced the same as the Carbonara, the Fish and Chips consisted of two batter-fried Modha fillets and a portion of crispy steak fries that were a beauty to behold. I do love good fries, and given that some places disappointingly offer regular skinny fries with fish and chips, this was very satisfying. It surprisingly wasn't too oily, and nothing was soggy — which is a win. One thing though, some parts of the fish had rubbery bits of fat in it that also exuded a pungent fishy odour which wasn't at all pleasant, so we just left those bits.

Aside from their main breakfast/ brunch section, the menu contains a "Sidekicks" and "Add-ons" bit. We got one out of each for curiosity's sake,  Homemade Bread with Hummus and Garlic Aioli (Rs. 390) from the Sidekick section, and Grilled Chicken (Rs. 120) from the other. From what we experienced, these are easily one of the best deals in the city.

You get a large platter of warm, freshly baked pita / kubbus-like bread topped with crumbs of cheese. It's light and fluffy, but unexpectedly filling especially when dipped in the smooth hummus and aioli.

We honestly don't know how this is a 120 rupees because it's SUCH a solid deal (fingers crossed that prices won't be jacked up soon!). You get two generous pieces of boneless chicken with a strong spice rub and marinade. The meat itself is succulent, flavoursome and lightly charred. Absolutely no complaints.

For drinks, we took a Lime and Mint Juice (Rs. 410) and a Blackbird Smoothie (Rs. 450). 

The Lime and Mint promises and delivers just that. It's a bit like a detoxing gotukola drink in how healthy it feels, so pour in approximately a tablespoon of sugar syrup from the tall little glass they give you along with your green juice. Now, for the Blackbird Smoothie. Concocted with a winning combination of yoghurt and peaches and spiced up a bit with some blackcurrant, this is a delicious fruit-flavoured yoghurt drink.

Conclusion

The range of options in the menu can be (and is) confusing if you're expecting just breakfast. They've got burgers, waffles, wraps, steak sandwiches, salads, muesli, English Breakfasts, Arabic Breakfasts, scotch eggs, and quesadillas to name a few. If mains aren't enough, they've also got desserts, smoothies, juices, coffees, and teas.

This is just our First Look, and we'll do a full-on review a few months down the line — but until then, we'd totally recommend it for a quick and filling meal.


TIP

Try their English Breakfast. It's great value for money.
