Cocoon Cafe is sadly closed now.

Cocoon Cafe is a new coffee-shop/hangout on Guildford Crescent (the Lionel Wendt road). As an extension of Cocoon Furnishing Fabrics it's really well furnished and quite chill to hang out in. The food we tried was nothing special, but as a place to hang out we think it's pretty cool.

The Location





We'll start with the location because that's the main draw. Guildford Crescent runs between Stafford International School and the Lionel Wendt theatre. It's one way and doesn't draw much traffic, which makes for a beautiful walking stretch. Cocoon Cafe itself looks out onto the road and a white picket fence on the other side. It actually feels like another, more hip city.



The interior is small, seating for about ten comfortably inside and six or so outside, but comfy. They have excellent furniture, all wood and cushions. There's also a rack full of cool magazines, books and games like UNO. It's a space you'd be quite happy to spend a fair bit of time in.

We detected a WiFi connection there, so if they give you the password it's a great place to work, meet people or just chill.

The Food





UPDATE: As you can see from the comments, we could be wrong about the food. We tried only a few things and they did have a very interesting and promising menu. We'll come back and try some more, if your experience is different please keep commenting and if you're reading, take that into account.

The food we tried was so-so. We tried the Spinach Feta Grill sandwich (Rs. 320), the Roast Beef sandwich (Rs. 400) and a Mango Frappe (Rs. 250). All fairly ordinary. The sandwiches were so so, couldn't really taste the feta and the bread wasn't quality enough to carry the dish. They didn't pull it through with any special condiments either. Basically unexceptional. Same for the roast beef. It didn't make Sri Lankan beef good and the overall impression was just meat and bread, nothing special. Even the drink just tasted like a mediocre mango juice.

They have a range of sweets and other foods that we haven't tried so comment if you have a different experience. Based on our first impression, we wouldn't go there for the food.

Service



It's a tiny shop and you're basically neighbors with the service staff, one person when we were there. They're quite helpful and nice.

Conclusion



Food is so-so, but atmosphere is excellent.

TIP

They have UNO and some cool magazines.
