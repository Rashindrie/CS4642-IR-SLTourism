Emperor's Wok is Hilton's Chinese restaurant. We dropped in to see how they matched up against Colombo's contemporaries. The food was not bad, but for what we ended up paying, it just wasn't good enough.

Food

The menu at Emperor's Wok is quite extensive so you'll a lot to choose from. The menu pricing doesn't seem so bad (small portion from Rs. 650 to Rs. 1,600) but you get slammed on the taxes and service. We ended up with an extra Rs. 1,500 on our bill - more than any single dish.

Overall we ordered five small dishes (enough for three) which came close to Rs. 7000 inclusive of taxes. Let's start with the base, their Yang Zhou rice (Rs. 860), a mixed fried rice with seafood and pork. What we liked was the fact that there was plenty of meat in the rice, but the let down was that it lacked seasoning. This was the general flow of the meal with most dishes, with the exception of a few lacking in some way or the other.

The Pei Pong cuttlefish (Rs. 1168) was the pick of the dishes we tried. It's a bit different from the usual HBC with thick batter-fried cuttlefish served with chilli and coriander. The batter was crunchy and seasoned well and the cuttlefish was cooked just right. Another nice dish was the pork belly (Rs. 1040), prepared very simply with soy sauce and chilli. While both these dishes were good, we still wouldn't say they blew us away.

The sweet and sour chicken (Rs. 860) was just plain sweet. While the chicken itself was cooked well, there was hardly any balance of flavour due  to the lack of any sourness.

The mixed vegetables (Rs. 860) was the most disappointing of the dishes we ordered. We noticed that the beans and carrots hadn't been cooked long enough and there was quite literally no salt added. We really wish we hadn't ordered this one.

Ambience & Service

Emperor's Wok didn't really impress with the service either. It was somewhat of a busy day, but we still didn't feel that it warranted waiting 45 minutes (we counted) for the food. So long in fact that we got hungry enough to finish all the pickle they put on the table, which didn't even taste that great. They've got two sets of waiters, in black and red. The ones in black were professional and had a good knowledge of the menu, the ones in red were just the opposite with the first guy forgetting to bring us a menu even after we asked him to. We had the same gripe as we did at the Thorana Lounge. If you're going to charge high prices and hefty service charges, the level of service should reflect that. In this case it did not.

The ambience here is pretty old school and elegant, which is what you would expect from a five-star hotel like Hilton. They've got some tasteful decorations like Chinese folding screens and artsy little chandeliers lighting the restaurant.

Conslusion

The food at Emperor's Wok is good in some ways, but just the opposite in others. When the cost for three comes to Rs. 7,000 the food needs to be of a very high standard, which we didn't think it was.

TIP

The people at the table next to us had brought a card pack to play while their food was prepared. We regret not having done the same.
