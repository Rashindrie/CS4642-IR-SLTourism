The Chocolate Room is an Indian franchise which has set up shop on Rosmead Place. It has a variety of chocolate based shakes, shots, drinks, desserts as well as savories and frozen yogurt. Sadly, the items we tried weren't that good.

The Food

The mainstays of any chocolate place would be the hot chocolate and the desserts. We started with one of the chocolate shots, a good idea at Rs. 180. This is literally a shot glass full of warm chocolate rimmed with sugar. There are different flavors, we tried the creme de menthe. It tastes nothing like that minty alcoholic drink, but it's pretty good. Just drinking a shot of chocolate. Bit sweet perhaps.

Sadly, the regular hot chocolate (in a cuddle cup, Rs. 400) wasn't so good. Quite watery and flavorless, actually. The hot chocolate at Cioconat is much better. This simply lacked taste and texture, and the cuddle cup looks a bit like a urinal.

Then the chocolate pancake (Rs. 500). Rubbery and unpalatable. Almost like a chocolate parata, but an old one. Not good. Finally we tried their frozen yogurt, which they call yogola (Rs 199). Tasted a bit funny - a bit like powered milk. Nothing was especially good.

The Location

Rosmead Place is a good street, but the interior of Chocolate Room is just a cacophony. The architecture is disjointed in the first place, being a converted home, but there are huge cuddle cups hanging from the ceiling, lots of text on the walls (some misspelled), and faux chocolate dripping from the walls. For us at least there's just too much going on. It actually makes it hard to focus.

Service

The service was quite good, but we were the only people there.

Conclusion

If you really like chocolate it's worth a try, but this Indian franchises drinks, sweets and frozen yogurt just aren't that good.

TIP

The chocolate shots are cheap (Rs. 180).

My experience couldn’t have gone any worse… We started out with drinks; I ordered the Pinky Lemonade and Shaz got the Ferrero Rocher Shake. After about 15 minutes, I got a yellow drink. Confused, I asked my server if my ‘pinky’ lemonade was supposed to be yellow. Maybe it’s just me, but I prefer my pink lemonade, well, pink. No, it wasn’t supposed tone yellow – instead he had made me a Piny Cooler - some concoction involving pineapples. I’m not a picky person, so I said it was ok, sucked it up and drank my pineapple drink. Honestly it tasted like pineapple cordial, which one can buy at any grocery store. Nothing special at all. Shaz’s shake was WAYYY to thick. If you can pick up your milkshake, turn over the glass, and not have anything spill, it is NOT A MILKSHAKE. It was a frothy excuse for a shake that was so thick, you could choke drinking it.

We couldn’t be bothered with the drinks and went on to the main course – Shaz got the Oven Roasted Chicken Sandwich and I went with the Nachos Grande. I’m a sucker for nachos, and the only nachos that come close to yum in Sri Lanka are the loaded nachos on the menu of Café Che, but I thought ‘what the hell, let’s give them a chance’. Big mistake.

Here I was, expecting “a large platter of tortilla chips covered with roasted chicken, hearty beans, warm cheese, and diced tomatoes, jalapenos, olives, pickled gherkins, and sour cream”. What I got was 15 tortilla chips with a spoon of beans and congealed cheese. Where was my roasted chicken? Where are my jalapenos, people?! Again, the waiter had gotten my order mixed up and put me down for the Nachos Supreme… which is the crap I just described. Never order it.

This time, I couldn’t settle, so I pointed out their mistake expecting quick service. Oh, it was quick alright. He walked into that kitchen and then right back out with a bunch of shredded chicken just put on top. Not wanting to risk sending my food back in, I asked for my jalapenos, olives, and sour cream separately. I got olives and gherkins. I didn’t even want the gherkins.

Shaz’s sandwich was good. I can’t deny them that. Soft, crunchy bread filled with a roasted chicken mixture that was moist and filled with flavor. The sandwich alone is worth another trip to this place… I’ll just go at 1 pm and have dinner there, eventually.

With an additional order of french fries, our bill came to Rs. 2,000 or something. I don’t even remember.

Having stated all of this, I believe that this place runs well when you order their desserts and other chocolate related items. The shake should have been better, but I think the cakes and stuff might be worth a try. Don’t take that statement to heart though, I don’t know if they’ll just start mixing the batter once you place the order.
