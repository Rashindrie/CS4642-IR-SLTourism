This place is now sadly closed. And it's open again! As Charcoal Gallery Cafe.

The intimate lanes of Mount Lavinia between the Galle Road and the sea comprise one of the city's most pleasant neighborhoods. An afternoon wander say from College Avenue to De Saram Place and then up Station road to catch the sunset at the rather magical Mt Lavinia Children's park is unfailingly pleasant. While Mt Lavinia is synonymous with the beach it's not just proximity of the sea that makes the area appealing- there's a wealth of handsome 40's and 50's era homes still surrounded by overgrown gardens, mature trees and a relative absence of traffic. This is one of the few places in the city you can walk without fear of fumes and well... sudden vehicle induced maiming.

Outside of school-rush hours, it feels like Havelock Town and sea-side Bambalapitiya did two decades ago when you still stood a chance of playing a game of cricket on Elibank Road. For all the loveliness though there are relatively few places, apart from residential homes, that really take advantage of this location. There is of course the beach side strip with Loon Tao, Boat House, La Voile Blanche etc but down the winding inside lanes there isn't enough to detain you. La Rambla has long been a pleasant exception however recently someone else seems to have noticed the neighborhood's potential and the promising Redwood Gallery/Cafe has come into existence.

Yup it's that Colombo classic cafe/gallery (think Barefoot and Paradise Road Gallery) combination. Redwood though is more intimate, low key and inexpensive than the other two. There's a small tea room, a larger gallery space and an attractive upstairs terrace with pretty outstanding sunset views. More than simply location however what really makes the place noteworthy is that it serves good coffee and above average tea and cakes for super reasonable prices. With Rs. 200 lattes and 250 cappuccinos Redwood, along with Hansa cafe, seems to advance the argument that coffee in Colombo can be good without breaking the bank. Along with our cappuccino we also had a plate of oven fresh scones, served with a blob of clotted cream and strawberry jam for Rs. 200 and a very good hot chocolate. This is important as decent hot chocolate is very hard to come by in Colombo.

Everything we tried was good and well priced and if the other items on the menu, cafe fare - waffles, burgers, sandwiches, all day breakfasts etc. keep the same standard this is one of the best value places in town. There's a lovely location, friendly service, good food and great coffee so we suggest you go there as soon as you can. Really.

In Colombo these small but promising places come and go, usually before they get the notice, attention and patronage they deserve, so seek out Redwood Cafe and give people who seem to be making a real effort a chance....

As a bonus you can walk around the neighborhood, up to the park and then saunter down to the sea after a good cup of coffee. La dolce vita, Colombo style.
