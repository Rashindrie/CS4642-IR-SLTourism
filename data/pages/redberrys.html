Redberry's is a little restaurant/ cafe that has been open for about two-and-a-half years now. Unfortunately, both the service and food border on mediocrity here. It's the sort of place where you go through most items on the menu only to be told that they're not available, and staff members lounge about and in the dining area carrying on personal phone calls. It's also the kind of place that does such a deplorably poor job that they have a happy hour smack in the middle of the day (from 3 PM to 6 PM or so).

Food and Service

The service was supremely slack and left us wondering whether they could do worse if they tried. The staff were off-hand, careless and overly casual, walking off halfway when we were saying something, with a wave of the hand to indicate that they'd be back. We were not pleased.

I wanted to try their chicken cordon bleu (not available), then their burgers (not available), then their shawarmas (also not available). Given limited choice, we then settled for boneless buffalo wings and an alfredo pasta. None of the items on the dessert menu were available either except for the ice cream, so we settled on drinks instead. My cousin ordered a chocolate blend (Rs. 450) and I went for a 'caramel' (which was a coffee blend). But surprise, surprise. I wasn't able to get that either, even though I had asked if it was available before ordering. After he confirmed that it was, I placed the order...and then he seemed to change his mind and asked if I could pick something else instead because their caramel (admittedly) wasn't all that great. (I have a feeling it wasn't available, but whatever).

The Chocolate Blend (Rs. 450) and the Redberry Blend (Rs. 470) arrived first, and they both tasted identically saccharine. I'm not sure what the Redberry blend consisted of, except that it tasted identical to the chocolate milkshake. The only difference between these drinks was the sugary syrup in the Redberry blend. There was a slight undertone of chocolate, but the only redeeming quality about these drinks was that they were refreshingly cold. 

The alfredo pasta (Rs. 450) fared somewhat better, although the mushroom sauce did look like congealed cheese.

It had some very flavoursome (MSG flavoursome) cubes of chicken, and a few stray button mushrooms drowned in dense sauce. On the bright side though, the pasta was surprisingly hot, so it wasn't too bad. The mushroom flavour came through quite strongly as well, and I'd give this a 2.5/5.

The boneless buffalo wings (Rs. 400), on the other hand, were a sorry, sorry sight.

They were dry and looked weather-beaten and droopy. They contained enough oil to make the Middle East pale in comparison, and enough salt to prevent iodine deficiency for generations to come.

Ambience

Chilled out, I guess, but it can't be anything but, given its great location on Marine Drive with a direct view of the sea. It's a pity that it isn't used well because the space in itself has so much potential. The interiors at Redberry's are all a light red or deep pink in colour — the only thing we could find in common with the name.

Conclusion

The total was just under Rs. 2,000 for two people, and it was an absolute waste of money. I'd strongly recommend not going here unless you really just want to check the place out.


TIP

Save yourself the trouble and don't go here.
