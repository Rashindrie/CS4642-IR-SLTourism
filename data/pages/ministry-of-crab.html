Ministry Of Crab (Dutch Hospital)

Dutch Hospital Shopping Precinct, Colombo 1

  • Call 0112342722

  • Closed right now

Ministry of Crab is a high-end restaurant on Dutch Hospital premises that specializes in crab cuisine - the food is just plain mouth-watering and the service is good.

Ministry Of Crab is probably Colombo's best restaurant. From quality of ingredients to character to location, this Dharshan Munidasa joint has it all. It even has celebrity co-owners in Kumar Sangakkara and Mahela Jayawardena. More than anything else, however, people come here for the crab. It ain't cheap, but it is delicious.

The Food

Min Crab's main innovation is in sourcing fresh ingredients, namely crab. Previously, the best Sri Lankan crabs were exported to Singapore and beyond. Now Dharshan keeps a good supply available here, though at a price. We ordered a Colossal Crab (1.2kg) for 5 people which came to Rs. 8,500 (1,700 per person). This was actually too much crab in the end but they were sold out of the smaller sizes. You can get medium sized crabs for around Rs. 4,000.

The crab itself is fleshy, succulent and delicious - a real treat. These are export quality crabs, what you get is significant amounts of crab meat soaked in your choice of sauce. We went for the new garlic chili sauce which was very nice. The classic is the Singapore style chili crab sauce, which is nice, but a bit sweet for my personal taste. They also have butter crab, which you need to order well in advance.

There's also some new stuff on the menu. They have fresh fish (grouper) for under Rs. 1,000 and clams and oysters. We tried the clams (Rs. 680). They came in a rich butter sauce and we had plenty of them. Good value, and we used the butter sauce for bread as well.

Another classic is the clay pot prawn curry (Rs. 2,480). This is prawn like you've never seen it, with two unfurled ones in there. It's a lot of prawn meat and a delicious hodi.

Definitely worth noting are the side dishes at Min Crab. This is the pol sambol, which is authentic and delicious. We also tried the amazing garlic and leek fried rices, kade paan (bread) and kangkun garlic and egg, but fingers were way too messy at that point to take photos.

We went with a vegetarian friend and (though we wouldn't recommend it) he was quite happy just feasting on the sides.

Overall the damage came to Rs. 19,000 for 5 people, or 3,800 per person. At about $30 USD this is quite a good value for tourists, which is who we went with. It is definitely expensive for locals, however, but you are getting consistently excellent value.

Drinks wise they serve an excellent Small Island Iced Tea - an arrack cocktail - for Rs. 400 as well as Hendricks gin and tonics and wine and beer.

Location

The Dutch Hospital is Sri Lanka's best new development and Ministry Of Crab is its centerpiece. You can dine on the verandah or inside the large communal style dining room (it has big tables where you seat near other guests).

It's a simple and beautiful space and completes the whole experience.

Service

The staff here is well trained, attentive, and modern - they take orders on iPod touches. It's a highly professional outfit and we've never had any complaints.

Conclusion

Dinner at Ministry Of Crab remains one of the best experiences in the city. It's really tasty, exciting and fun. Expensive, but we highly recommend.


TIP

Go for the chilli crab.
