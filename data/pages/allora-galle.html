Allora is closed for the off-season, till about August they say.

Smack in the middle of Sri Lanka’s prettiest colonial areas, the Galle Fort, Allora is an absolute delight. Serving a range of Italian food and located on the cross of good old Pedlar’s Street and Lighthouse Street, the restaurant is a good spot to grab a nibble while on an artsy walk around the historic Fort.

While dinner is always a great option, something about the simple café ambience really lends itself to a lazy lunch. They are also a tiny hop and skip away from a whole host of dessert places – Barista, Pedlar’s Gelato and Pedlar’s Inn Café. As for the name, an Italian friend translated it to “so”, which seems pretty vague to me.

Food





Allora serves a good selection of Italian fare – simple, very tasty, with no superfluous frills on the menu or plate. Their pastas are incredibly filling and richly flavoured, with the carbonara getting a special mention (being authentic and whatnot, it comes with bacon so make sure to order something else if pork isn’t your deal). The tomato and basil salad was small, but still a steal for Rs. 350, and was dressed with a rather fun vinaigrette. They’ve also introduced a new specials board with a bunch of new options including a bruschetta plate with either tomato, porcini or truffles and a chicken cannelloni which looked worth a try!

Other options aside, their pizza is the best choice if you want something filling but not heavy. They do a delicious thin-crust home made pizza, baked fresh in a oven on their premises. The smell wafting around is reason enough to order it. Their choices range from seafood (my personal staple) and tandoori (which I had no inclination to try. These cultural melting pot shenanigans should be left to Pizza Hut) to classics like margarita and pepperoni. They also try out a couple of new toppings every couple of months and seem fairly flexible so you can request a mix and match if you want.

The tables come equipped with chilly flakes for the Lankan patrons, and a super chilly-infused oil which I recommend you slather liberally on everything!

Drinks



Allora serves a house white and red and an extensive range of fresh juices which are fairly delicious and averagely priced. If you ask the waiters nicely, they will bring you cans of Lion beer for about Rs. 300 each which is a bit pricey but well worth it on a sweltering Fort afternoon. Also nothing beats a gourmand pairing of pizza and beer.

Ambience





Yet another converted colonial bungalow, Allora has an almost authentic European vibe with their hardwood floors and red and white checked tablecloths. The Fort setting, whitewashed walls and large, open windows make you feel as though you’re in a sleepy Italian village for the summer. Their walls are adorned a little more deliberately, with quirky movie stills of people eating spaghetti and those ubiquitous vintage movie posters. They have varied seating, which serves well for couples, families and trestle tables for large groups.

While the simplicity is refreshing, be prepared for the heat in the afternoons. There’s no AC and the gently whirring fans do really add to the languid ambience, but expect to sweat if you sit on the veranda.

Prices & Service





One of their pizzas is generally big enough for two people, depending on your appetite and extent of sharing-is-caring attitude. So at about Rs. 1200 a pop, it works out reasonable. Their pastas are just under Rs 1000 each, but are very filling and seem to use great ingredients. I’m not entirely convinced I’d pay Rs. 850 for a Caesar salad, but maybe regular salad enthusiasts would disagree.

Their service is fairly effective, although at lunch time there appears to be only one waiter lurking about. Although the food is all made fresh on the premises, they manage to be fairly quick. However, while the food is brought post-haste, you can expect to wait a good 10-15 minutes plus for your bill, and have to throw in some strenuous hand movements to get the lone waiter’s attention too. At night, it’s a little more crowded so there’s more staff to handle everything.
