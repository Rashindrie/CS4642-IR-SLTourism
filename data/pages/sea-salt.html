UPDATE: This place is closed

Just a tiny air-conditioned alcove on the crazily busy Galle road, Sea Salt is an easily overlooked place that serves up brilliant, fresh seafood. Literally a hole-in-the-wall, there's barely any place to sit or chill (better for take-away/delivery), but the food is well worth it.

Food & Drinks

The fish meat is white, flaky and perfectly done inside
The menu was pretty extensive, with a good selection of varied seafood and even a couple of vegetarian options. The Sea Salt guys go to Peliyagoda fish market early every morning for the day's catch, and prices vary slightly accordingly. When we went, the prices were as following for each 100 gram portion: Seer fish at Rs. 250, garoupa at Rs 180, thalapath at Rs. 220, mullet at Rs. 180, prawns at Rs. 300, and cuttlefish at Rs. 200. It pays off, because the fish and prawns were just great. We ordered 350 grams of batter-fried prawns at Rs. 1050, which came with a mayonnaise and a nice home-made sweet chillie sauce. This was quite possibly the best dish out of a great meal, with the right batter to prawn ratio, perfect crispiness and mild spice. We also tried a deep fried whole garoupa fish which came to Rs. 1080 for 600 grams. I'm generally not a fan of whole fish, because it can be unnerving to see the original form of something you're ingesting, but it was quite perfectly done. The outer skin was crispy and crackling, soaked nicely in a sweet-spicy garlic sauce, with soft white meat inside. We topped the two dishes off with a seafood nasi goreng, which serves as a good base. Despite having chunks of fried/dried chillie inside, it wasn't too spicy. There were also generous bits of seafood mixed in - cuttelfish, fish, and prawns all fairly represented. We initially thought Rs. 320 was a bit expensive, but the portion was more than sufficient for three people so it turned out to be fine. Drinks-wise, they've gotten inventive with some variations on classics, but they're really not that great for the prices. We ordered a lime twist and green mist, both at Rs. 150, and a chocolate milkshake at Rs. 180. The lime twist was essentially sprite with a couple of lime slices thrown in, and the green mist tasted exactly like liquid gummybears with additional sugar syrup. The chocolate milkshake was alright, although it didn't have enough chocolate flavouring in it.

Service & Ambience

The service is as efficient as possible, as you have their full attention. The manager is usually around so he recommends the best stuff on the menu, along with appropriate sizes and side dishes. He even offered lots of varieties of sauce depending on our preferences. The food also arrived within 10 minutes, which is great as it was all freshly cooked (we could hear the spicing and frying process begin in the kitchen). The ambience isn't that snazzy, it's just a tiny little shop on a road that looks like it's an active war zone. Hopefully Galle Road is all spruced up and cleared away soon, as these guys plan to have outdoor seating for dinner once that's done. For now, expect lots of rubble and traffic and sun and not much space. It was just three of us but we were pretty squashed, and ended up pretty much eating standing up. Luckily the food was good enough to distract us. The seating for now consists of bar stools against the wall, and the decor involves a fair bit of orange and neon. And some zoomed in wallpaper of fruits.

Conclusion

This is a super place to grab a quick seafood lunch for one or two people. It's tiny so better suited for delivery or take-away. The food is fast, fresh, and perfectly done, in our experience. Altogether it came to about Rs. 1000 each for food and a drink.


TIP

There isn't much seating, so don't go with a crowd unless you're getting take away.
