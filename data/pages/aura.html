Aura Café

04, Buller's Lane, Colombo 07

  • Call 0766 388 788

So... we gave this place a rough review last time. I went back again, in many aspects, we were wrong. Note that we weren't really there for a review, plus we were a bit scared to take photos... so no photos.

Aura Cafe is a really innovative use of space down Bullers Lane which serves up an ambitious menu that almost makes it. The food ideas are good but the execution is still about average. Given that they have a engaged and excellent staff and an obvious sense of design, we think it'll evolve and grow. This new spot is worth a try.

Ambience

I'll start with ambience (the scene) because that's the best thing about Aura Cafe. The first thing you notice is that there is parking. This is sooo rare for Colombo restaurants which are mostly re-purposed houses with two parking spots. Not that you should be driving (take a PickMe), but if you are, Aura actually has space for you to park. Which is great.

Why do they have parking? Because of a very innovative use of space. Instead of a building, they have a simple two-story container structure and a well-manicured garden. So most of the land is available for use. The interior is well-lit and decorated, but we decided to sit in the garden, which is why I have no pictures, because it was dark. Le sigh. Hopefully words will suffice.

Basically, we love the architecture and design, it's a really modern concept for a restaurant. Very simple and focused on what matters. The quiet and peaceful location down the residential Bullers Lane (opposite Rupavahini) doesn't hurt.

Food

Now on to the main bone of contention. Our last review went on a bit about high prices and small portions, and in their defence we did order some of their more expensive dishes. I added their (mercifully short) menu to our online system, so you can look through it yourself. I'd say that we were wrong to complain about price, but the portions could be more generous. Some stuff is expensive, but the overall range of Rs. 550 to 2,950 is basically normal.

Also, if restaurants want to charge more, that's fine, even necessary. For cuisine to really improve, certain ingredients, staff and experiences just cost more, and they can be worth it. At Aura three of us ate happily for under Rs. 6,000, including juices and dessert. If you order right you can eat here for less than Rs. 1,000 ahead, so they're not really out of line.

Juices

The juices (from Coco Ceylon) are actually not cheap (Rs. 400 to 500) and they do come in small bottles, but they're really really good. If we measured fruit density these would score quite high, the Coco Berry really felt like drinking a smoothie solid with berries. I'd say these are good value.

Starters

My mother, being my mother, ordered a soup as her main, the Sicilian seafood soup (Rs. 800). This soup looked beautiful, like a yellow Italian tom yum with saffron broth, but the taste never really rose above average. It was neither aggressive nor subtle. By aggressive, I mean there could have been more punchy acidity from the tomatoes, or the shellfish stock could have been stronger (using more shrimp heads and shells for flavor). Instead you got something that looked and sounded beautiful but tasted normal. Honestly, in a less nice ambience this may have felt better, but everything else at Aura actually sets expectations rather high.

Mains

For mains I got the pork ribs (a daily special, around Rs. 1100 I think). They came with a sweet, dark oriental sauce (is that racist?), pita bread and salad. This is actually billed as an appetizer but, yeah, I guess I am my mother's son.

Because there are so few ingredients on the plate, the each have to be well balanced and amazing. In this case the pork was ample enough for me and tender and cooked well. So far so good.

For me, however, the sauce lacked the acidity to balance its sweetness. Maybe I'm just an acidity fan-boy, but for me a good plate of Asian-style ribs mixes sweet with a bit of punch from vinegar or Chinese wine or soy (or whatever). Honestly, it doesn't matter how you get it, but for me the sauce could have used some excitement to elevate. For me a plate of savory/sweet ribs is all bass and I could use a bit of treble. 

The salad and pita bread were fine, but there was nothing done to really elevate them either. They were just sorta there.

For the other main we tried the pork schnitzel (Rs. 1,100). Again, focus on the breaded pork with minimal sides, so the protein really has to sing. In this case also, it was breaded and cooked perfectly, with tender pork and the right amount of crunch. What it lacked was either a strong flavor of its own, or a sauce to complement it. Could have been a bit less oily also, but the main thing was that that it arrived without cream sauce at all. Which was mentioned on the menu, and I think necessary for the dish. There was a bit of chutney type thing, but basically there wasn't anything to make the pork more interesting.

The mashed potatoes were textured and nice, but the brocolli was just sort of there. 

So basically, overall, the food here is very promising but the execution needs a little something extra to max the tight design vibe of the place. Personally, I think the cooking is done perfectly and the portion size is fine. The sauces and seasoning could improve to take it to that next level. Honestly, in another context this food would be fine, but this place has the potential to be better.

Desserts

The desserts are outsourced quite intelligently to Bakes By Bella. The jars are small but full of sweety goodness, be it that banana banoffee or whatever else is on offer that day. We love B by B and it's worth going here just to try her desserts. 

Service

The service here is great. Best thing is that there are actual women waiters. At most places in Colombo you're only served by men, which is weird. Feels a lot more comfortable here. The staff seems to be friends and family and they're obviously invested in the place.

That more than anything means that this place will evolve and grow. They seem to have a good, smart team and we're confident that the food will take it that extra 5% to make this place legitimately awesome.

Conclusion

Aura Cafe is a truly innovative restaurant with decent food. They've used their space on Bullers Ln really well (containers FTW), created a beautiful dining area and have capable, engaged staff. The menu is focused and creative and with a little more work we think it'll be excellent.

This is a good addition to the neighborhood. We're sorry we were a bit hard on it before.


TIP

Good for dinner.
