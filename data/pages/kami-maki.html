Kami Maki is a Japanese fusion restaurants in Thimbirigasyaya. While it's not the best Japanese in town, they do offer some interesting fusion dishes.

The Food

When Kami Maki opened up opened up around a year ago, they were quite popular because of their Japanese fusion theme. Now, however, the crowds seem to have dried up a bit. Their menu is quite unique in that it doesn't have as many traditional Japanese options, instead they offer a pretty good selection of Japanese inspired fusion dishes. Prices, while not too cheap, aren't as high as other Japanese eateries, so managing under Rs. 1000 is possible.

First off we had tried out their sushi - tuna, white fish and shrimp nigiri (Rs. 190 each). This was disappointing, plain and simple. The fish wasn't very fresh, there was a clear lack of finesse in knife-work and the rice was overcooked. The soy sauce helped to balance out the flavours a bit, but the texture of the rice was too hard and grainy.

After a pretty bad start we weren't expecting much with their tenkasu crab with wasabi mayo (Rs. 690 for 6), but this one turned out to be a lot better overall.

Even in this case the rice wasn't all that great, but there were good elements to overcome that. What made this one stand out was the wasabi mayo which had plenty of kick to it combined with the sprinkled sichimi, and a bit of crunch from the sesame seeds. 

The teriyaki beef rice burger (Rs. 750) was pretty good, as long as you don't expect an actual burger. Instead of bread this has rice buns, which are actually pretty sturdy, but still hard to pick up. The beef was also cooked well with the flavour of the teriyaki sauce coming through nicely and the caramelized onions added a good amount of sweetness. One thing we can't complain about is the portion size, which was plenty for one person. 

For dessert we tried their cookie coated chocolate ice cream (Rs. 400). It's not fancy but it's actually very satisfying. The biscuit coating also adds a nice little crunch.

Ambience & Service

Kami Maki is actually a pretty small place that on the corner of Jawatte and Thimibirigasyaya. It's not really the best place for hanging out since the triangular space can get pretty cramped up.

The service was fine. The waiters were friendly, attentive and knew the different dishes and preparations when we asked them. The only issue is that if you're sitting upstairs, the waiters aren't always around, but they do have a bell. They're also one of the few Japanese eateries that offer delivery.

Conclusion

Kami Maki is definitely different from the norm, and if that's what you're looking for, you'll be glad you dropped in. It's not great for sushi, but their fusion dishes are interesting, as are their desserts.


TIP

The rice burgers and rolls are your best bet.
