Kebabish is a little eatery that serves up shawarma, biriyani and kottu in Dehiwela. While they've never got around to offering up their namesake kebab, they used to have a reputation for their biriyani. However, what's really good there right now is the shawarma. We swung by and grabbed some takeaway for dinner.

Food

What's great about Kebabish is that you won't break the bank eating here as all the items on the menu are under Rs 400. They've got a dolphin kottu for just Rs 280, coming up to maybe Rs 350 if you add cheese to it, which is a good deal. At lunch time you'd probably want to get their biriyani, but after 6 PM you can try their naan with curries.

We ordered a shawarma to go, and watched as the chef delicately prepared it. It takes a good 10 minutes to make the shawarma, mostly because of how carefully this guy works at it. If you watch, you can see him pick up each element of the shawarma one by one and place it onto the roti. It's all done so neatly that you feel the love this guy has for this dish.

While it doesn't come close to the likes of Arabian Knights, the shawarma at Kebabish is a humble creation that sates your cravings. It's got a bit of crunch from the cucumber, and just enough mayo to make the filling creamy but not so much that it spills out. There's a generous amount of chicken in there. Overall it's not too spicy and a decent bite. A large chicken shawarma is Rs 350, though it's only one inch bigger than the regular 6" at Rs 300.

We also asked for a chicken masala (Rs 180) and some garlic butter naan (Rs 80). While the chicken curry was cheap, the portion was so small you could call it a handful, with just a whole lot of onions and no gravy. Ideally, you want so much gravy that you keep grabbing at naan after naan, but that's not the case here, and we were disappointed.

Still, the chicken is tasty enough but it's a pretty one dimensional flavour of a savoury masala. You only get one piece of chicken so it's meant for a single person.

Ambiance & Service

Kebabish isn't much for ambiance. You never really feel like eating there. It's a cramped space with capacity for 8 or 12 people, and it's not air-conditioned so it's not super comfortable. The service is just okay. They won't go out of their way to make recommendations. It's a pretty straightforward process of just making your order, telling them if it's to go or to eat there. The man making the shawarma seems nice, though.

Conclusion

While their shawarma is good, nothing else really makes much of an impression at Kebabish. Their menu caters to single servings, so it's not the place you want to go to if you want a large biriyani. They do deliver if you're around the area, so if you're feeling the munchies and want to chow down on some shawarma, they're the one to call.

 


TIP

Just go for the shawarma.
