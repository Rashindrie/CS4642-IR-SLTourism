Kinnaree is a contemporary Thai cuisine restaurant behind the Dutch Hospital that's been around for just about two years, now. They've got a great location, but don't seem to pack too much of a crowd. With a Thai chef in the kitchen, you would expect some authentic dishes being plated up, but that wasn't quite the experience I had when I had lunch there on a Sunday with my brother and my girl.

Ambience

We quite liked the spaciousness of the place. There's a lot of free space and daylight flooding in through the windows. If you're with a group of friends and just want to chill out with some beer and watch a match, you can go further upstairs where they've got beer towers and a wide screen tv. From the main dining area, you can see the kitchen through glass walls. At lunch time on a Sunday, when we were there, though, the widescreen was playing some Sinhalese teledrama that one of the staff seemed to be watching.

Food

First off, the menu at Kinnaree can be approached in three different ways. You can either go the usual route of picking your noodle or rice and then your side dish, or you could try their One Plate meals, or you could try the Kinnaree curries. This abundance of options doesn't really help you make a decision, though, but on weekdays you've got set lunches so you won't need to bother so much with that. 

We hit the Gold Bag starter (Rs 850) which is a finely minced chicken and shrimp ball wrapped in wonton skin and then deep-fried. This delectable treat is served up with chili sauce and tastes great with or without it. You can't tell the difference between the two proteins, and it mostly tastes like prawn, but that doesn't take away too much from its overall awesomeness. You get 6 wontons that you can share with two to three people. Not bad.

Following the starter, we tried the Glass Noodle Salad (Rs 780), though the Caeser Salad on the menu caught us off guard, as well. I guess those menu options are for the guests who were brought along but didn't really want to eat Thai food? The menu is rife with a lot of these random items from other cuisines. Anyway, back to the glass noodles. What a disaster. We tried the minced pork and the pork didn't even taste like meat anymore. Why? Because this entire dish was overpowered by sickeningly sour lime juice. From the noodles to the onions to the chillies, every single note on this dish was sour. Bad scene.

Unfortunately, we had ordered limesoda (Rs 220) and iced tea (Rs 160) for drinks, and those didn't help wash out the taste at all. On the bright side, every table comes with a litre bottle of water, so we went at it, hoping that the rest of our order would make up for this. The lime soda was alright. Iced tea at Kinnaree's doesn't really taste so much like what you're used to, though. My girlfriend found it odd, and when I tried it I thought I tasted siyambala. Maybe tamarind is a contemporary take on the drink or something. Who knows. Definitely not the waiter.

Out came the One Plate, just in time to save the day. We asked for the green curry chicken (Rs 780) and shared it amongst the three of us. The rice portion was just perfect for someone like me who doesn't go too heavy on the carbs, and was cooked right, like a sticky basmati. Green curry, though, failed quite badly. My brother mostly picked on its lack of greenness, and we all agreed that the coconut milk overpowered any other flavour on this dish. The only other taste coming through was the kafir lime leaves, and that's also only if you bit into it. The chicken was shape, so. How to complain? It was a creamy sauce chicken with a bowl of rice. If you ate too much of this, you'd get a tummy ache. If you leave 80% of the curry in the bowl, you're safe.

Finally, the real saviour arrived. At Rs 920, this small portion of pad thai was the best part of our meal just behind the Gold Bag starter. The flavours were balanced, as you would expect from Thai cuisine. The prawns were not overcooked and tasted fresh. The noodles were well-seasoned though just a little on the overcooked side. The portion is really just enough for two people. If you're really hungry, you could easily gobble one of these by yourself. I'd recommend this with the Gold Bag and then desserts, or booze. Then, again, when we were there, all they had was ice cream, despite a fairly decent range on their menu. Oh, well.

Service

The waiter who served was quite charming in his hot pink Thai print holiday shirt, but he was really clumsy when it came to placing cutlery on our table. He wasn't very good with making recommendations based on the menu, either. That being said, he was mostly nice and actually asked us how the food was and showed concern when we hadn't finished the glass noodles. We told him how we felt about the green curry and he responded defensively, saying it's a Thai chef and that's how Thai food tastes. We were also the only customers there at the time, so his reluctance to take feedback after asking of it was a bit jarring.

Conclusion

Kinnaree is a nice place to go to watch a match or something. There's a wide screen that you can watch from upstairs where they've got their beer towers and such. It's not the best Thai restaurant in town by any stretch of the imagination. At Rs 4,800 for three people, with no dessert, it's a little pricey. We were lucky that there was a debit card discount, so we got Rs 666 cut off our cost. Go here if you're having drinks at Dutch and want a good bite a la Gold Bag. Maybe try the business lunches if you work around here.

 


TIP

Hit the 25% wine deal. Gold Bag starters are bomb.
