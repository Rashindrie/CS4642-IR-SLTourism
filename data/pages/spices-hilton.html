Spices (Hilton)

Hilton Colombo, Sir Chittampalam A Gardiner Mawatha, Fort, Colombo 01

  • Call 011 2544644

  • This place has closed down

Spices dabbles in Sri Lankan, Indian, and Western Cuisine. They're popular for their buffets, but have an a la carte menu as well.

Spices is a fusion of Sri Lankan, North Indian, and Western cuisine, which also has a few East Asian (cue Japanese) options. If you want to be pampered by great service and tweak around with the buffet and a la carte menus (because they're flexible like that), this is where you need to be. Prices are on the high-end, but then again, this is at the Hilton.

Food and Service

Spices' buffet is famed for having great meals, but we opted for the a la carte menu because though we take our meals quite seriously, neither Imaad nor I can give a buffet the justice it deserves. The stewards are very welcoming and friendly, and they stretch the menu out for you, allowing you to switch between the buffet and a la carte options — like for instance, we were told we could order a la carte, but could help ourselves to unlimited bowls of soup and tiny loaves of bread laid out for the buffet. They have a dessert buffet option as well, so you can, you know, binge out on like 30 varieties of dessert laid out instead of paying for the whole buffet. We didn't opt for that because (a) quite a lot of it was fruit and (b) they didn't have cheesecake or apple pie. Both of those were available on the a la carte menu though, but more on that later.

We helped ourselves to a bowl each of the Soup of the Day (Rs. 580), which happened to be brown onion soup. This was a very subtle, caramelized onion concoction, where the sweetness of the onions came out just so. It was rather oily, but in a very light and subtle way. We liked it, and it went well with the bread presented to us.

Imaad then went for a Chicken Chorizo (Rs. 880) and I ordered a Chicken Tikka Masala with Naan (Rs. 1200). Both are rather exorbitantly priced, especially when you consider that the Chorizo was rather dry and the mash which accompanied it was chunky, with barely any butter or milk to smoothen it out.

On the plus side, the sausages were quite flavoursome, so it wasn't a complete disappointment.

We'd recommend sticking to the Indian or local options because they seem to be much more satisfactory. My Chicken Tikka Masala was gloriously coloured and flavoured, with generous pieces of chicken in the depths of the gravy.

The naan was also soft and warm and went perfectly with the gravy. While it isn't spicy to the Sri Lankan palate, it isn't bland either; this dish is catered to suit both South Asian and Western palates, with the spices balanced just so.

The New York Cheesecake (Rs. 650) is the same as the one from Thorana Lounge. It comes with a thick cream cheese body, and no base or toppings.

When I'd asked about the toppings, I was told it's a plain cheesecake (despite the menu stating otherwise), but one of the other stewards later told us to help ourselves to any of the toppings laid out on the buffet table, so that went great (really).

We tried it out with chocolate sauce and a couple of fruity toppings, and then we had a lot more cheesecake left, after which we tried it out with the pol-pani toppings. Mind you, these were the toppings kept aside for the pancakes.

The clear winner was pol-pani. Why isn't this a combo as yet? Cheesecake with pol-pani is the new waffles with pol-sambol. This needs to be a thing.

Ambience

It's a bit too cold, with the aircon decked up a bit, but the space is nice, with wicker-woven chairs and pine-coloured chairs. There's plenty of natural light thanks to a glass wall overlooking a lawn. It's a good place to unwind and have some good food.

Conclusion

The food here is pretty delicious and the staff are amazingly friendly and accommodating, but the food is quite expensive. We'd recommend going for the buffet lunches (Rs. 2,700 per person), because a la carte costs pretty much the same amount for less food. And try the pol-pani with your cheesecake.


TIP

Try their cheese cake with pol-pani
